I am an aspiring HCI researcher preparing PhD applications in Human–Computer Interaction, with a focus on human–AI collaboration, intelligibility, and evaluation. I study how design choices and systems constraints shape trust, performance, and equity when people work with adaptive models. My broader goal is to build interactive tools that make AI understandable, controllable, and reliable in real workflows.

Research Interests

  • Human–AI collaboration and mixed‑initiative interaction
  • Intelligible/transparent AI: explanations, uncertainty, and control
  • Evaluation methods: calibration, workload, error recovery, and fairness
  • Prototyping & experimentation: end‑to‑end interactive systems
  • Participatory design and values‑centered HCI
  • Systems pragmatics for usable ML (latency, logging, privacy‑aware data flows)

I aim to design interfaces and workflows that help people form accurate mental models of AI systems and effectively steer them. My PhD research will combine interaction design, controlled and field evaluations, and systems‑minded prototyping to advance practical, trustworthy human–AI collaboration.
