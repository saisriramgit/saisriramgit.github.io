Bio

I am an aspiring HCI researcher preparing PhD applications in Human–Computer Interaction, with a focus on human–AI collaboration, intelligibility, and evaluation. I study how design choices and systems constraints shape trust, performance, and equity when people work with adaptive models.

Broadly, I hope to explore the intersection of Human–Computer Interaction, explainable AI, and decision support. My long-term goal is to design AI systems that help people interpret model behavior, manage uncertainty, recover from errors, and meaningfully guide adaptive models in high-stakes or everyday contexts. Through controlled studies, interface experimentation, and systems-minded prototyping, I aim to advance practical, trustworthy human–AI interaction.

Research Interests

My research focuses on advancing human–AI collaboration by designing interactive systems that make intelligent technologies more transparent, usable, and trustworthy. I study how people form mental models of AI behavior, especially when systems are opaque, uncertain, or adaptive, and how interface design can help users interpret, steer, and collaborate with these models.

I am interested in three connected directions.

  • Designing interaction techniques and explanation mechanisms that improve intelligibility and user control.
  • Studying how people integrate algorithmic suggestions into decision-making through controlled experiments and real-world deployments.
  • Developing participatory methods that enable users to meaningfully shape and influence the behavior of AI systems.
